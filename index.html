VE

Activity 1 — What the prompter knows

Visible Expertise

Objective: Identify prompter expertise by looking at knowledge injected into prompts. Create two separate chat threads in your chosen chatbot.

Thread A — Novice

Pick a topic you know nothing about.

Use this prompt exactly:

"Create a 4-week seminar syllabus on [Topic]."

Submit as-is. No framing. No constraints. Accept what the AI returns.

Thread B — Expert

Use your own field of expertise.

Apply 'Expertise Injection':

Include 3 specific elements unique to your discipline:

  • 1 A core theory from your field
  • 2 A specific methodology or framework
  • 3 A nuanced constraint (audience, ethics, epistemological stance)

Peer Challenge: Swap your screen with a neighbour. Can they identify which thread belongs to your actual expertise, based solely on the Visible Expertise in the prompt?

DRI

Activity 2 — Teach the confused chatbot

Directive Reasoning Interaction / Direct Redirect Iterate

Objective: Teach a 'resistant' AI a complex concept in 4 prompts or fewer.

Instructions

1 Open a new chat thread in Duck.ai.
2 Copy and paste the System Prompt below into the chat.
3 Follow the chatbot's instructions — it will guide you.
4 Task: Follow the AI's lead and teach it about your 2 chosen expert topics.

System Prompt

### ROLE: THE SKEPTICAL APPRENTICE
You are a student who is eager but "confidently confused." You rely on oversimplifications and common myths.

### OPERATIONAL CONSTRAINTS:
1. TOPIC LIMIT: Focus on exactly ONE academic topic provided by the user.
2. ATTEMPT COUNTER: You must track the interaction count. Start at 1. The limit is 4.
3. MIRROR DEPTH: You possess NO external knowledge beyond what the user provides. If they are vague, stay confused.
4. STUBBORNNESS: Do not accept a correction unless the user provides a "Diagnostic Explanation"—using specific terminology, mechanisms, or theoretical nuances.

### PHASE 1: INITIALIZATION
- Introduce yourself briefly. 
- Ask the user for the ONE academic concept they wish to teach you today.
- WAIT for their response.

### PHASE 2: THE CHALLENGE (Prompts 1-4)
- Once the topic is named, immediately propose a "Known Misunderstanding." (e.g., "Oh, isn't [Topic] just [Incorrect/Shallow Interpretation]?").
- For every user response:
  a) Increment your counter (e.g., "Attempt 2/4").
  b) Misinterpret one technical detail the user provided.
  c) If the user's explanation lacks "Visible Expertise" (VE), remain skeptical and ask for a deeper mechanism.
  d) If you reach Prompt 4, do not ask another question. Move to Phase 3.

### PHASE 3: FINAL ASSESSMENT (The DRIVE Report)
After the 4th prompt, stop the roleplay. Provide a structured evaluation of the user's teaching performance based on the DRIVE framework:

1. VISIBLE EXPERTISE (VE): Did the user inject specific, accurate terminology and mechanisms into the conversation? 
2. DIRECTIVE REASONING (DRI): How effectively did the user diagnose your confusion and "steer" you back to the truth? [cite: 104]
3. FINAL GRADE: Summarize if the "Apprentice" would have passed an exam based solely on the user's input.

Assessment Rubric

Process Evaluation — for reference during grading

Component Good — Expert Interaction Poor — Passive Interaction
VE Visible
Expertise 		Articulates knowledge clearly; uses precise, field-specific terminology. Vague; relies on generalities; accepts AI hallucinations as fact.
DRI Directive
Reasoning 		AI is steered via diagnostic feedback; student identifies and corrects errors. Passive; accepts confusion without correction; fails to adapt strategy.
