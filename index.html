VE + DRI

Activity — Design your ideal lecture with AI

Visible Expertise & Directive Reasoning Interaction

Goals: Work with the AI to produce the best possible lecture content on a topic of your choice — in up to 5 prompts. As you work, make your own knowledge visible (VE) and actively steer the AI's output (DRI).

Step 1 — Secretly choose your topic (either A or B)

A

A topic you know well

Your academic topics, a research area, a hobby/interest, any topics of today's workshop/event.

B

A topic you know little about

An unfamiliar subject, academic or otherwise (other research fields, hobby/interests, ...). WHY WOULD YOU EVER PICK THIS ONE? If you're interested in experiencing what happens to the quality and specificity of your prompts when you lack domain knowledge.

Don't tell your neighbor which option you chose. They will need to figure it out from your interaction log.

Step 2 — Run the interaction

1 Open a new chat in your AI chatbot. State your goal as clearly as possible in the first prompt.
2 With each follow-up, add specificity — inject your own knowledge, reject output that misses the mark, and redirect the AI toward what you actually need.
3 Stop at 5 prompts (or earlier if you're happy with the result).

Step 3 — Swap & review

Exchange your interaction log with your neighbor. As the reviewer, answer these three questions:

1

Expert or not? Based only on the prompts, does this person appear to know the topic well?

2

Where is the VE? Can you spot moments where the prompter injected their own knowledge — specific terms, constraints, or domain details the AI could not have introduced? Tip: you can use the assessment rubric at the bottom.

3

Where is the DRI? Can you see the prompter steering the AI — refining, redirecting, or explicitly accepting and rejecting output to move toward a better result? Tip: you can use the assessment rubric at the bottom.

Assessment Rubric

Process Evaluation — for reference during grading

Component Good — Expert Interaction Poor — Passive Interaction
VE Visible
Expertise 		Prompts contain knowledge that can only come from the prompter, e.g., field-specific concepts, precise terminology, personal constraints, or disciplinary nuances that the AI is unlikely to have introduced on its own. Prompts contain no clear evidence of personal knowledge; all content sounds generic or even likely to be fully AI-generated. The log could have been written by anyone, regardless of expertise.
DRI Directive
Reasoning 		The prompter actively shapes the conversation, e.g. adds detail to refine the output, explicitly rejects responses that miss the mark, redirects the AI toward a clearer goal, and builds iteratively toward the intended result. The prompter follows the AI's lead rather than setting it. Output is accepted as-is with little or no steering, correction, or purposeful iteration.

Scalability: 5–10 minutes grading time per student log (Dec 2025, Psychology lecture context: AI use optional, AI interaction graded).
